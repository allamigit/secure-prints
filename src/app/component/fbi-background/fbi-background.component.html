FBI Background Check

How it works:
1. Request:
Individuals can request their own criminal history summary by completing a written request and submitting fingerprints to the FBI's Criminal Justice Information Services (CJIS) Division.
2. Fingerprinting:
Fingerprints are required to verify identity and access the criminal history record. Ink fingerprints can be obtained at local police departments or some private fingerprinting companies.
3. Processing:
The FBI compares the fingerprints against its database to determine if there is a criminal record.
4. Report:
The FBI provides a report summarizing the individual's criminal history, often referred to as a "rap sheet".
5. National Instant Criminal Background Check System (NICS):
The NICS is a separate system used by licensed Federal Firearms Licensees (FFLs) to check if an individual is legally eligible to purchase a firearm.
Who needs an FBI background check?
- Individuals seeking a copy of their own criminal history.
- Individuals applying for certain licenses or jobs that require a national background check.
- Law enforcement agencies for various purposes.
